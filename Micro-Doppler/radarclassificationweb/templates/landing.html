MICRO DOPPLER BASED TARGET CLASSIFICATION

About the Project

Introduction Micro-Doppler classification is a technique that leverages the Doppler effect to analyze subtle motion patterns from various targets, such as people, animals, or objects. By using sensors like radar or LiDAR, the system captures these fine-grained motion features, which can be instrumental in differentiating between various types of movement. How It Works The core principle behind micro-Doppler classification is that moving objects reflect radar signals in a manner that is influenced by their velocity and the nature of their motion. Applications Micro-Doppler classification is widely used in various fields, including: Security and Surveillance: Identifying suspicious activities or detecting intruders based on motion patterns. Traffic Monitoring: Classifying vehicles and analyzing traffic flow. Wildlife Monitoring: Tracking animal movements without direct observation. Search and Rescue: Locating individuals in disaster scenarios through motion detection
